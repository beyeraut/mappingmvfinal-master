SALVAGE ARCHAEOLOGY

Here you can see salvage excavations taking place on both the western and eastern sides of the highway.

The Morton Village site was discovered in the 1980s during a highway expansion project. During this salvage excavation a small portion of the village site was removed to prevent the complete loss of data for that section of the village as the result of the highway construction. This kind of excavation is referred to as ‘salvage excavation’, which differs from the current archaeological research project in that an entire portion of a site is removed to save it from destruction and complete loss of data, while the current research project focuses on careful and selective removal of portions of the site for scientific research.
